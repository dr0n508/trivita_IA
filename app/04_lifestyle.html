Asymptomatic lifestyle-related conditions are known to cause chronic inflammation. The term asymptomatic means there are no symptoms associated with the risk factors. Over time, these types of conditions increase the incidence of chronic inflammation and exist long before you have any indication of chronic illness.

Lifestyle

1. How many times per week do you exercise 30 minutes or more? (walking, jogging, hiking, yoga, bicycling, treadmill, resistance, etc)

2. Do you snore?

3. How much time are you sitting during waking hours?

4. How much pure water do you drink per day? (not including tap water, tea, coffee, juice or any flavored drinks)

5. Do you drink 2 or more alcoholic beverages per day?

6. Do you smoke?

7. Are you around other smokers or live in a home with a smoker?

8. How would you rate your day to day stress level?

9. When did you last see your Primary Care Provider for an annual physical?
